1992 Los Angeles riots — Wakapedia
The 1992 Los Angeles riots, also known as the LA uprising, were a series of civil disturbances beginning April 29, 1992. Six days of unrest resulted in 63 deaths, over 2,000 injuries, approximately 12,000 arrests, and roughly $1 billion in property damage — the deadliest riots in American history since the 1967 Detroit riots, sparked by the acquittal of four LAPD officers who beat Rodney King.
