ORION Health Overview

Hearing voices or sounds after a car accident is a recognized clinical phenomenon with several possible explanations. This is sometimes called post-traumatic auditory hallucination and may be associated with:

  • Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD) — Intrusive auditory memories triggered by trauma, especially in accident survivors
  • Acute grief response — Auditory experiences of a deceased person's voice are reported in 50–80% of bereaved individuals
  • Stress-induced perception disturbances — Heightened sensory sensitivity under extreme psychological stress
  • Traumatic Brain Injury (TBI) — Even mild concussion can produce temporary auditory disturbances
This is general health information only. If you are experiencing persistent hallucinations, please consult a licensed medical professional.
Featured result

Auditory hallucinations following traumatic events are far more common than often assumed. Studies indicate that between 30–50% of trauma survivors report some form of intrusive auditory experience in the weeks following the event. These are usually temporary and diminish with proper support and processing.

Is it normal to hear a deceased person's voice after they die?
Yes. Research consistently shows that 50–80% of bereaved individuals experience some form of post-bereavement hallucinatory experience. Hearing the voice of the person who died is the most commonly reported form and is typically brief and non-distressing.
Can a car accident cause auditory hallucinations?
Yes — through traumatic brain injury (even mild), acute stress response, PTSD, or the combined shock of trauma and loss. Most cases resolve naturally. Persistent symptoms warrant evaluation by a neurologist or psychiatrist.
What is the difference between grief voices and psychosis?
Grief-related auditory experiences are tied to a specific loss, are usually recognizable and non-threatening, and diminish over time. Psychotic hallucinations tend to be persistent, commanding, unconnected to a specific loss, and accompanied by other symptoms like paranoid ideation.
How long do post-traumatic auditory hallucinations last?
Most cases resolve within 3–6 weeks as the acute stress response diminishes. Grief-related auditory experiences may persist longer but typically decrease in frequency over the first 3 months. Cases lasting beyond 6 weeks or causing significant distress should be evaluated clinically.
